Personal Statement

I realized I could feel the same silence in completely different ways. The first time, I didn’t understand it. The second time, I did. That difference changed the way I see myself in social situations and how I react to them now.


At the beginning of high school, I was genuinely excited. I expected to find close friends and feel understood. During the first semester, I met people and spent time together, but something felt off. Conversations didn’t excite me and instead of feeling connected, I felt distant.


Months later, at a party full of noise and distractions, I experienced that same silence again. But this time, I realized something important: silence is not always rejection. Sometimes people are distracted, environments are chaotic or timing simply isn’t perfect.


That realization changed me. I started questioning negative assumptions and became more aware of reality instead of my fears. I learned that confidence grows through actions, not thoughts.


Today, I continue developing socially while understanding that silence does not define me — my actions do.
